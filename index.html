Frequently asked

Do I need to know Hebrew or Aramaic?

Not at all. The Daily Dose is written in clear English. Hebrew and Aramaic terms are explained in context. Many of our most loyal readers have no formal Jewish education or language background.

What if I miss a day or fall behind?

Don't worry about it. Most Daf Yomi readers miss days. The schedule waits for no one, but the archive is always here, and the next email always lands tomorrow. Consistency over perfection.

How long does each day take?

The Daily Dose is a five-minute read. Studying the actual page in depth typically takes 45–60 minutes, but that's optional — the email alone is meaningful.

When does the current cycle end?

The 14th cycle began January 5, 2020 and ends around June 7, 2027 with a global Siyyum HaShas celebration. The 15th cycle begins immediately after.
